# Healing from decades of narcissistic abuse.

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/5EmIf8zyTbM" title="Healing from decades of narcissistic abuse." frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Brooke Brodack addresses a question her fans have asked for years: why did she leave the beloved Brookers character behind? The answer is raw and deeply personal. After receiving comments asking her to bring back the comedy persona, Brooke sheds light on the very real pain that was hidden behind that character. While some people use comedy to heal, Brooke has chosen a more conscious path through mindfulness.

She reveals that someone closest to her talked behind her back for most of her teenage and adult life, telling family members and friends she was "crazy" and urging them not to help her. The damage was systematic -- good friends disappeared from her life without explanation, and she had no idea why. This secret sabotage destroyed her self-worth, her confidence, and ultimately her career. The person responsible still does not see what they have done wrong and continues viewing Brooke as the problem.

Working with an NPD (narcissistic personality disorder) specialist over the past two years, Brooke has finally been able to see her own narrative clearly. She understands now the triangulation and scapegoating that was done to her and how deeply it undermined her ability to succeed. As much as she loves Brookers, she has made a deliberate choice: healing from decades of emotional and verbal abuse and helping others do the same is a far more valuable way to spend her time.
