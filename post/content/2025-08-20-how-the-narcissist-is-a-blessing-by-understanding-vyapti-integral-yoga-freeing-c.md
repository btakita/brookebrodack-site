# How the narcissist is a blessing by understanding Vyapti (integral yoga) & freeing creative blocks.

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/yUDx2zt31BA" title="How the narcissist is a blessing by understanding Vyapti (integral yoga) and freeing creative blocks." frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Brooke Brodack explores the concept of Vyapti from integral yoga in a format that includes guided commentary, revealing a profound insight: if you do not exercise your Vyapti, you will receive the imprint of other people's Vyapti. In a world full of narcissism, this realization carries enormous weight. It means that creative blocks, writer's block, and stagnation are not personal failures -- they are often the result of absorbing others' influences without conscious awareness.

The video offers a practical yogic framework for breaking through creative blocks. The approach involves three steps: first, dissolve all existing ideas and preconceptions, making the mind completely clear and open to every possibility with equality. Second, shift your level of consciousness inward -- away from the physical surface mind into the subtle or subliminal ranges. Third, shift upward, opening to higher intuitive inspiration. When these steps are combined, the creative flow can begin spontaneously, sometimes arriving as a torrential outpouring.

This is a deeply practical video for anyone -- writers, artists, content creators -- who struggles with creative blocks. Brooke connects the dots between narcissistic influence, Vyapti, and the yogic tools available for reclaiming one's creative power.

## Key Topics

- Vyapti from integral yoga and how it operates between people
- The connection between narcissism and creative blocks
- A three-step yogic framework for dissolving writer's block
- Shifting consciousness inward and upward to access intuition
- The intermingling of influences and becoming conscious of them
- Choosing to allow or refuse external influences
