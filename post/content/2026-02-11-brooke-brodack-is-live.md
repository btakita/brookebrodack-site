# Brooke Brodack is live!

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/x9LW86QaARM" title="Brooke Brodack is live!" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" allowfullscreen></iframe>

In one of her most candid and vulnerable live streams, Brooke Brodack opens up about a difficult day that brought her back to the fundamental truth she keeps rediscovering: no one can fill your cup for you. After skipping meditation for two days, she found herself shouting at someone, burning out, and spending hours scrolling YouTube searching for something -- or someone -- to bring her clarity. The answer, as always, came when she sat down for an hour of evening meditation.

Brooke shares a powerful insight about the two categories she sees in the world: full-on monks who are detached from lay suffering, and everyday people running on autopilot. She positions herself in between, as a lay meditator trying to balance family responsibilities with a dedicated practice. The revelation that hundreds of people tuned into her silent meditation live streams moves her deeply -- she sees it as proof that people are "craving peace and silence and deep inner knowing" over personalities and egos.

The live stream also touches on her fruitarian diet transition, the upcoming astrological shift from the year of the snake to the year of the fire horse, and her evolving relationship with YouTube after nearly two decades on the platform. She candidly reflects on how her early YouTube fame shaped her identity and how she is finally letting go of basing her value on others' opinions. The dogs interrupt multiple times, adding genuine humor to an otherwise deeply reflective session.

## Key Topics

- The critical importance of daily meditation for mental health and clarity
- Seeking identity and joy outside yourself versus going within
- The power of silent meditation live streams -- hundreds tuning in to sit in peace
- Transitioning from consumer to creator mindset through meditation
- Brooke's evolving relationship with YouTube after nearly 20 years
- Fruitarian diet and the astrological shift into the year of the fire horse
- Balancing lay life responsibilities with a dedicated spiritual practice
