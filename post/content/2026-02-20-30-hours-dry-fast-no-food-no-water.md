# 30 hours dry fast no food no water.

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/VLs_IjgaDh0" title="30 hours dry fast no food no water." frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" allowfullscreen></iframe>

At the 30-hour mark of her first-ever dry fast, Brooke Brodack delivers one of her most contemplative and philosophical videos. Speaking with a calm, measured energy, she describes a state of "slowness" and "stillness" that has allowed her to stop being ahead of herself for the first time. She shares that people have been spontaneously connecting with her and offering jobs -- a phenomenon she attributes to the alignment and clarity that comes from genuine self-care.

Brooke opens up about spending thousands of dollars over the years trying to heal issues that turned out to be inflammation, problems that a simple diet change and fasting could have addressed. She references a breatharian rapper who proposed that when cells break down during fasting, they release hydrogen which combines with inhaled oxygen to create water internally -- questioning whether humans might be more autonomous than conventional wisdom suggests.

The video reaches its emotional peak as Brooke calls on viewers to "take back our time, take back our lives," urging people to stop being "entertaining monkeys for the masses" and instead explore ancient fasting practices. Her sign-off -- "May all beings be happy. May all beings be liberated" -- reflects the deep spiritual grounding she has found through this challenging practice.

## Key Topics

- The 30-hour dry fast experience: slowness, stillness, and unexpected clarity
- Tens of thousands spent on medical bills that could have been solved with diet and fasting
- The theory that fasting cells create internal water through hydrogen and oxygen
- Breatharianism and questioning human dependency on external food and water
- Breaking free from cycles of consumption and entertainment culture
