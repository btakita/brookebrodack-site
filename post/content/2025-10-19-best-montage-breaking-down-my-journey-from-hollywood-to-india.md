Brooke shares a compelling montage from her appearance on the Cullum Flynn Podcast, tracing her extraordinary journey from YouTube's earliest days to spiritual transformation in India. The clip recounts how a hyperactive 19-year-old from Holden, Massachusetts became the most subscribed YouTuber in the world with 60,000 subscribers in 2005 -- a massive achievement at the time. NBC offered her a development deal, The New York Times called her YouTube's first star, and she moved to Hollywood to live with fellow emerging creator iJustine.

Then came the twist that defined her story: she deleted her channel and all social media, and moved to India. The montage weaves together archival footage, podcast narration, and Brooke's own reflections, including her pivotal 2012 spiritual experience and her evolving views on Hollywood and social media. As she puts it, "YouTube is about you sharing your own dream -- at least getting out of Hollywood's dream." It is a fascinating capsule of internet history and one creator's courageous choice to walk away from fame in search of something more authentic.
