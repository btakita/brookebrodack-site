# Divine intervention, exploring intuitive nature

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/TidSu3UQr2E" title="Divine intervention, exploring intuitive nature" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Brooke Brodack dives deep into the nature of intuition and inner perception in this extended conversation with a spiritual teacher well-versed in the teachings of Sri Aurobindo and the Mother. After Brooke shares her experience of sensing catastrophic events before they happen, including visions related to the Asheville, North Carolina flood, the teacher draws an important distinction between the psychic being (the true soul's consciousness) and surface-level occult perceptions that are often mislabeled as "psychic powers."

The discussion reveals that inner visions of future events represent possibilities, not certainties, and that many predicted catastrophes throughout history have been modified or averted by greater spiritual forces. The teacher references a 1980s book called "We Are the Earthquake Generation" as a cautionary example of well-intentioned but ultimately unreliable predictions. Rather than chasing these perceptions, Brooke is advised to prioritize spiritual growth and use inner sight as a secondary tool, always filtered through quiet intention and connection to the Divine Consciousness.

The conversation offers practical guidance for anyone interested in developing intuitive perception: quiet the mind, set a conscious intention that acts as a filter, wait patiently for insights to unfold without grasping at them, and hold the state of the vision rather than fixating on the image itself. The teacher emphasizes that the state carries more information than the image and that spiritual growth should always take priority over developing occult faculties.

## Key Topics

- Distinction between the psychic being and surface occult perceptions

- Sri Aurobindo and the Mother's teachings on inner sight

- The supramental force and its role in averting catastrophes
- Quieting the mind and setting conscious filters for perception
