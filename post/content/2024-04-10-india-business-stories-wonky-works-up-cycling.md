# India business stories. Wonky works up-cycling

<iframe width="100%" style="aspect-ratio:16/9" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/FMCAnrlw2H4" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Brooke Brodack shares the colorful story of Wonky Works, an upcycling business run by her friend Ekta in Vadodara, Gujarat, India. The twist: Gujarat is a dry state, making the alcohol bottles that served as Ekta's raw material technically illegal. Using a huge kiln in her warehouse, Ekta would reshape these bottles into beautiful vases, table toppers, magnet vases, napkin holders, and cups, turning contraband into art.

Brooke recalls working briefly on a social media campaign for Wonky Works, though the warehouse conditions made it challenging. The space was shared with a neighbor doing heavy metal construction, creating an almost comically noisy work environment. She brainstormed ideas with Ekta including a "message in a bottle" conservation campaign, which they ultimately made digital to avoid putting trash in the ocean.

The video paints a vivid picture of India's youth-driven business culture, where pop-up cafes and trendy shops targeting college students can make quick money before the fickle demographic moves on to the next thing. Brooke reflects on the rise-and-fall cycle of Indian small businesses with characteristic honesty and affection, noting that Wonky Works has since closed but that she treasured her time collaborating in Ekta's incredible warehouse space.
