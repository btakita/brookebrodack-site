Brooke Brodack brings her comedy chops to the forefront with this hilarious skit-turned-cooking-video. She plays "Betty-Karen," an entitled customer at a smoothie drive-up who demands a coconut chocolate smoothie with ashwagandha. When the employee tells her she can come in and make it herself, the surprise twist is that Betty-Karen actually does -- and discovers it is way better that way. The video cleverly flips the Karen trope on its head with a wholesome, empowering message about self-reliance.

The second half transforms into a genuine smoothie-making tutorial as Brooke walks viewers through her recipe: vegan protein powder, coconut milk powder, organic bananas, ashwagandha, spinach, and water. She shares that ashwagandha means "sweet horse" in Hindi and advises using it sparingly due to its potency. The whole process is filmed with one hand while keeping an eye on her baby, adding an authentic layer of new-mom multitasking to the production.

The video lands its message with charm: making things yourself means you get exactly what you want, including the healthy additions you might not think to order. Brooke proves that comedy and wellness content can blend together just as smoothly as the ingredients in her blender.
