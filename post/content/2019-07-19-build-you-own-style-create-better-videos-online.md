# Build you own style (Create better videos online)

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/DWmzBDFnoQI" title="Build you own style (Create better videos online)" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Brooke Brodack goes live from Bali -- her first stop after leaving India -- to share her perspective on the future of YouTube and announce her creative consulting services. Fresh off months of travel through India, she explains why she prefers live streaming over pre-recorded content: the laptop microphone captures better audio than her iPad's 4K camera, and audio quality is the single most important element of good video content.

The video touches on a LinkedIn message asking Brooke to predict the next three years of YouTube. Her answer: more honest, timely, synchronistic, and fresh content. She recalls working with Sarah Austin on Pop 17 years ago when Austin predicted live would be the future -- a prediction Brooke now sees coming true. The rest of the stream introduces her creative coaching services, emphasizing inspiration-based guidance for YouTubers and traditional artists alike, and her work teaching people personally how to vlog and create meaningful content.

## Key Topics

- Audio quality as the most important element of video creation
- The future of YouTube: live, honest, and timely content
- Creative consulting and coaching for YouTubers and artists
- Choosing Bali after leaving India based on a viewer comment
- Sarah Austin and the early prediction that live content would dominate
- Finding your unique content creation style
