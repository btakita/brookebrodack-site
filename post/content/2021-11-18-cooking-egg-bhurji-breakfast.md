# cooking Egg bhurji breakfast

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/-83E77eA1WM" title="cooking Egg bhurji breakfast" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Brooke Brodack invites viewers into her RV kitchen to cook her favorite breakfast: egg bhurji, an Indian scrambled egg dish she picked up during her years living in India. Filming one-handed after her gimbal dies, she walks through the recipe with warmth and humor, starting with a garlic ginger paste and building up layers of flavor with onions, tomatoes, coconut oil, cayenne red pepper, and turmeric, all served on ghee-toasted bread.

The cooking process has its share of entertaining mishaps -- eggs get dropped on the floor, the gimbal fails, and Brooke juggles filming with cooking in a tiny RV kitchen. But these moments are what make the video feel genuine and relatable. She describes the dish as an Indian-American fusion since she makes it her own way, noting that the garlic ginger paste makes an enormous difference compared to simply tossing raw vegetables into the pan.

Brooke rates her bhurji an 8 out of 10, wishing she had added more cayenne, and invites Indian viewers to share what they call this dish in the comments. The video wraps with a tease of her next cooking video: poha, a flattened rice and potato dish, along with a humorous aside about how teaching English online has made her hyper-aware of pronunciation.
